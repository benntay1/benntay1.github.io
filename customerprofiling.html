Customer Profiling and Segmentation

This project was done as part of my module curriculum at NUS.

Module code: DSA3101 Data Science in Practice
Techniques used: Market Basket Analysis, Customer Profiling and Monetary Value Analysis

This project focusses on data science and business analytics tchniques to extract actionable insights from data. The dataset we used was a private dataset from an online sales business, capturing data on item sales, calorie nutrition values and collected details on customers who bought the respective item.

Our team's objectives were to increase the client's revenue in a socially responsible and sustainable manner, by introducing novel and personalised business recommendations targetting both the management as well as the consumers.

Our business recommendations were from a 2-pronged approach: (1) association rule mining and (2) item substitutions.

Association Rule Mining

Through ARM, we analysed the client's dataset and generated rules with confidence > 0.60, these rules were then targetted at customers of a higher income value. We made this decision through monetary value analysis, in which this group of customers contributed to over 40% of the client's annual revenue for 2019.

Item Substitutions

The second part of our approach deals with targetting customers who are of a BMI other than 'healthy'. Overweight customers are then given item recommendations based on their income levels as well, to ensure that social welfare is taken into consideration.

Similarly for underweight customers.

Evaluation

Through preliminary calculations and assumptions, we estimate the client's annual revenue to increase by 14%.
